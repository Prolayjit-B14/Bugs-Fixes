Introduction

Raag Bhairavi is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early morning and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Bhairavi belongs to the Bhairavi Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Rishav, Komal Gandhar, Komal Dhaivat and Komal Nishad.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Bhairavi is performed during the **late morning (9 AM - 12 noon)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S r g M P d n S'

Avaroh: S' n d P M g r S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): Ma

Samvadi (Second most important note): Sa

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

M g S r S, d' n' S r S

Mood & Emotion

According to Indian classical vocalist Pandit Jasraj, Bhairav is a "morning raga, and solemn peacefulness is its ideal mood." It is grave in mood and suggests seriousness, introversion and devotional attitude.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a sampurna (complete) raga, meaning all seven notes are used in both ascent and descent.
  • Komal Rishav, Komal Gandhar, Komal Dhaivat and Komal Nishad is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
