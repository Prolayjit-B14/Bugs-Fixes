Introduction

Raag Darbari Kanada is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early morning and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Darbari Kanada belongs to the Asavari Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Gandhar, Komal Dhaivat and Komal Nishad.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Darbari Kanada is performed during the **midnight (12 AM)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S R (M)g, M P (n)d n S'

Avaroh: S' (n)d n P M P (M)g, M R S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): R

Samvadi (Second most important note): P

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

g M d n d P g M R S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Darbari Kanada is a majestic, emotive raga known for its grave and solemn character, often evoking feelings of seriousness, introspection, and a sense of devotion. It's derived from the Natabhairavi melakarta and is commonly used in both Hindustani classical performances and film music.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a sampurna (complete) raga, meaning all seven notes are used in both ascent and descent.
  • Komal Gandhar, Komal Dhaivat and Komal Nishad is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
