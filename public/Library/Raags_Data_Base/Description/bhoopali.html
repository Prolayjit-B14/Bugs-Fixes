Introduction

Raag Bhoopali is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early evening and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Bhoopali belongs to the Kalyan Thaat, characterized by the omit of Ma and Ni swaras.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Bhoopali is performed during the **early evening (6 PM - 9 PM)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S R G P D S'

Avaroh: S' D P G R S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): Ga

Samvadi (Second most important note): Dha

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

G R S D' S R G P G D P G R D' S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Bhoopali evokes a mood of **devotion, peace, and serenity**. It is considered a "shuddha" (pure) raag and is often associated with spiritual compositions.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a aurav-aurav raga, meaning only 5 notes are used in both ascent and descent.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
