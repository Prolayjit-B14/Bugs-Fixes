Introduction

Raag Kedar is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early evening and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Kedar belongs to the Kalyan Thaat, characterized by the use of Teevra Madhyam (sharp Ma).

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Kedar is performed during the **early evening (6 PM - 9 PM)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S M, M P D P, N D S'

Avaroh: S' N D P, M' P D P, M R S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): Ma

Samvadi (Second most important note): Sa

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

M P D P M, S R S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Kedar evokes a mood of **devotion, peace, and serenity**. It is considered a "shuddha" (pure) raag and is often associated with spiritual compositions.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a Aurav-Sampurna (complete) raga, meaning 5 notes in ascent and all seven notes are used in descent.
  • Teevra Madhyam (sharp Ma) is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
