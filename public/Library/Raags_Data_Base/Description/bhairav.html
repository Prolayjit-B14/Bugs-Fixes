Introduction

Raag Bhairav is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early morning and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Bhairav belongs to the Bhairav Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Rishav and Komal Dhaivat.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Bhairav is performed during the **early morning (6 AM - 9 AM)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S r G M P d N S'

Avaroh: S' N d P M G r S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): dha (Komal Dhaivat)

Samvadi (Second most important note): ri (Komal Rishav)

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

d P G M r S

Mood & Emotion

Bhairav is typically performed with a peaceful, serious, and serene mood. In classical Hindustani language, its rasa is "Shaant aur Gambhir".

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a sampurna (complete) raga, meaning all seven notes are used in both ascent and descent.
  • Komal Rishav and Komal Dhaivat is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
