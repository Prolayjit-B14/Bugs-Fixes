Introduction

Raag Desh is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early evening and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Desh belongs to the Khamaj Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Nishad.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Desh is performed during the **night (9 PM - Midnight)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S R M P N S'

Avaroh: S' n D P M G R G S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): R

Samvadi (Second most important note): P

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

R M P n D P M G R G S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Desh evokes a mood of **spirit, nature, peace, and serenity**. It is considered a "shuddha" (pure) raag and is often associated with spiritual and earthly compositions.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a Auurav-Sampurna raga, meaning 5 notes are used in ascent and all 7 notes in descent.
  • Komal Nishad is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive, romantic feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
