Raaga Identified ⓘ Raaga is the melodic framework of Indian classical music that evokes specific emotions.
Yaman
Raag Yaman is one of the most fundamental ragas in Hindustani classical music, known for its serene and peaceful mood. It is performed during the early evening hours.
Key Notes:
Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni
Taal Identified ⓘ Taal refers to the rhythmic cycle in Indian classical music that organizes time and beats.
Teentaal (16 beats)
Teentaal is the most common taal in Hindustani classical music. It consists of 16 beats divided into four groups of four beats each.
Beat Pattern:
Dha Dhin Dhin Dha Dha Dhin Dhin Dha Na Tin Tin Na Ta Dhin Dhin Dha
Emotional Essence ⓘ Each raag is associated with specific emotions or moods called 'rasa' in Indian aesthetics.
Shringara (Love) & Shanta (Peace)
This performance primarily evokes feelings of tranquility and divine love. The serene progression of notes creates an atmosphere of peace while the melodic ornamentations suggest romantic devotion.
Technical Analysis ⓘ Detailed analysis of the musical techniques and structures used in the performance.
  • Predominant use of meend (glides) between Ga and Re
  • Strong emphasis on tivra Ma (sharp 4th)
  • Madhya laya (medium tempo) with occasional drut (fast) passages
  • Complex taans (fast melodic sequences) in the latter sections
  • Characteristic phrases of Raag Yaman clearly established
