Introduction

Raag Malkauns is one of the most fundamental, oldest and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early morning and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Malkauns belongs to the Bhairavi Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Gandhar, Komal Dhaivat and Komal Nishad.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Malkauns is performed during the **late night (3 AM - 6 AM)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: S g M d n S'

Avaroh: S' n d M g M g S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): Ma

Samvadi (Second most important note): Sa

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

M g M d n d M g S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Malkauns is often said to evoke a state of severe tranquility. Hear how Pandit Nikhil Banerjee's sitar opens an austere realm of contemplation - the mood is mournful yet always stable, as if finding resolution whilst meditating on some great loss. Its five-note scale is simple, but there is no other raga like it.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a Aurav-Aurav raga, meaning five notes are used in both ascent and descent.
  • Komal Gandhar, Komal Dhaivat and Komal Nishad is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
