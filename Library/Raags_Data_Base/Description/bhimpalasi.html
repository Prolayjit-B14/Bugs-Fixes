Introduction

Raag Bhimpalasi is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Bhimpalasi belongs to the Kafi Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Gandhar and Komal Nishad.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Bhimpalasi is performed during the **late morning (9 AM - 12 noon)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: n' S g M P n S'

Avaroh: S' n D P M g R S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): Ma

Samvadi (Second most important note): Sa

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

n' S M P g M g R S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Bhimpalasi is a Hindustani classical raga known for its mood of quiet longing, serene dignity, and deep emotion, often associated with the late afternoon/sunset time period.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a Aurav-Sampurna raga, meaning 5 notes are used in ascent and all 7 notes are used in descent.
  • Komal Gandhar and Komal Nishad is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
