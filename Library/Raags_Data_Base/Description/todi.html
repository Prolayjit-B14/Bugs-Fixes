Introduction

Raag Todi is one of the important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early morning and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Todi belongs to the Todi Thaat, characterized by the use of Komal Rishav, Komal Gandhar, Teevra Madhyam, Komal Dhaivat.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Todi is performed during the **late morning (9 AM - 12 noon)**.

Mood & Emotion

Raag Todi, a Hindustani classical music raga, is known for its ability to evoke a range of emotions, from melancholy and pensive moods to moments of playfulness and frolic, and is often associated with a sense of profound expressiveness.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a sampurna (complete) raga, meaning all seven notes are used in both ascent and descent.
  • Komal Rishav, Komal Gandhar, Teevra Madhyam and Komal Dhaivat is a defining characteristic.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
