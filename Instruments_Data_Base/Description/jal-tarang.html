Jal-Tarang

Origins and History:

The Jal-Tarang is an ancient Indian percussion instrument that produces sound using water-filled ceramic or metal bowls.

Instrument Features:

It consists of multiple bowls filled with varying levels of water, struck with bamboo sticks to create melodic tunes.

Musical Role:

Used in classical, folk, and fusion music, the Jal-Tarang produces a soothing and ethereal sound.

Conclusion

The Jal-Tarang remains a unique and mesmerizing instrument in Indian classical and experimental music.
